# Product

## Register

product

## Users

High school students (grades 9–12, ages 13–18) researching competitive programs, internships, and scholarship opportunities. They're ambitious, often applying to multiple programs simultaneously, and want a tool that takes them seriously — not a patronizing edtech toy or a government portal from 2012.

## Product Purpose

A curated directory of 50+ programs, internships, competitions, and scholarships for high school students. Success looks like a student finding and tracking an opportunity they would have otherwise missed — and actually applying.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, credible, uncluttered. Serious students using a serious tool. The product should feel like it was built by people who respect the users' intelligence.

## Anti-references

- No edtech pastels: no bubbly rounded everything, no Duolingo/Khan Academy cheerfulness, no cartoon-friendly gradients.
- No generic SaaS indigo: no indigo-purple gradient on every button and header, no #F8FAFC cream background, no identical shadow-card grids.
- No government/nonprofit dullness: not a scholarship portal from 2012, not bureaucratic, not visually timid.

## Design Principles

1. **Tool over toy.** Every visual decision should reinforce that this is a real product for students with real goals. No decoration that doesn't carry information.
2. **Typographic clarity first.** Hierarchy comes from scale and weight, not color noise or emoji substitutes.
3. **One color, used intentionally.** The brand color appears on primary actions and key state changes — not on every button, label, and heading.
4. **Content is the signal.** Filter labels, badges, and status indicators earn their place by adding information, not by adding visual texture.
5. **Confidence, not persuasion.** The design shouldn't need to convince you it's good — it just is. No "⭐ FEATURED" ribbon gradients, no "FOR STUDENTS, BY STUDENTS" eyebrows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Support reduced motion. Keyboard navigable.
